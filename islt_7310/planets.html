The Planets

If we think of the signs as the “how” in our lives, we can think of the planets as the “what”; planets have different functions or roles in different areas of your life and the sign a planet is in can indicate how that area of your life may be expressed.

It’s important to note that Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto are what we call “generational” planets- everyone in your generation will have those planets in the same sign, since they move so slowly. The sign they’re in is less important to you as an individual, but can give insight to common traits among a generation. When considering how those planets affect you individually, it’s more important to look at what house they’re in.

Sun: Planet of Self

  • Represents one’s personality and ego
  • Creative ability and how you meet challenges of everyday life
  • Your conscious mind, reason opposed to instinct
  • May be the way people describe you to others

Moon: Planet of Emotion

  • Rules over your emotions, inner world, and subconscious
  • The way you nurture others and how you want to be nurtured
  • Our personal needs, basic habits, and spontaneous reactions
  • Shows us how we respond to our environment and where we may feel most at “home”

Mercury: Planet of Communication

  • Communication, intellect, awareness, manner of thinking, reasoning
  • Rules the way we speak to others or our style of writing
  • The way you handle nervous energy
  • Coordination of ideas and sensory information

Venus: Planet of Love & Money

  • The type of items you own, how you spend your money
  • Our sentiments, values, where we derive enjoyment
  • Approach to relationships, how we attract others
  • Arts, love, romance, entertainment, comfort

Mars: Planet of Passion

  • Instinct for aggression, anger, and survival; action over reaction
  • Our drive, desire, and active energy motivating us internally
  • Where power, courage, or confident expression comes from
  • What makes you angry, how you express that anger

Jupiter: Planet of Luck

  • How we express our generosity and tolerance
  • Where you may find good fortune, or may overindulge
  • Philosophical and spiritual views, sense of purpose
  • Your approach to learning, what experiences provide wisdom

Saturn: Planet of Karma

  • Where you have a need for self-control, boundaries, and limits
  • How we approach our responsibilities
  • The obstacles you have to overcome to find success
  • Discipline, managing time, work ethic

Uranus: Planet of Rebellion

  • The area of life you tend to rebel in
  • Pushing past limitations, liberation, chaos
  • Where you seek the most freedom and creativity

Neptune: Planet of Illusion

  • Spirituality, creativity, mystery
  • Where you have the most intuition
  • How you connect with yourself

Pluto: Planet of Power

  • The area of your life you search for deeper meaning
  • Where the most change may occur
  • What you have issues of control and power with

Now that we’ve gone over signs and planets, we can start looking at a few examples to get a better understanding of how they work together!

Mercury in Libra: You can recognize this placement in someone who speaks with great diplomacy; they may be able to connect with others easily, charm their way through disagreements, and diffuse tense situations with their communication skills. You’ll often notice these placements struggling with indecision, playing devil’s advocate, and seeking compromise.

Mars in Aries: These placements move fast! They can be quick to anger, and even quicker to move on. They’re able to make decisions quickly based on instinct, using spontaneousness as an asset. They can come off as impatient or blunt, as their nature goes against beating around the bush and they seek a fast pace to keep excitement rolling.
