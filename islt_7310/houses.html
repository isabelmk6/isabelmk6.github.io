The Houses

Going with our previous theme of “how” and “what”, houses make up our “where”. There are 12 houses in astrology, and each is associated with a different important area of life. The planets and signs you have in a house speak to the themes you’ll experience in that area.

1st House

  • House of self; the sign this house is in is considered your rising sign
  • Beginnings, self, identity, outward appearance
  • The first impression you make on others
  • Personality, early childhood, essential qualities

2nd House

  • House of self; the sign this house is in is considered your rising sign
  • Beginnings, self, identity, outward appearance
  • The first impression you make on others
  • Personality, early childhood, essential qualities

3rd House

  • Communication; thinking, talking, messaging
  • Reflects the community you grew up in and early education
  • Relationships with siblings or neighbors
  • Defines the way you think, process, then share

4th House

  • Foundation of you chart, represents the home
  • Reflects our ancestry, home life, and family
  • What kind home you want to create; what you need to feel safe/comforted
  • The way you nurture others and your approach to self-care

5th House

  • House of self-expression and creativity
  • How you can express yourself and have fun
  • Our childlike spirit and how we give to our children
  • The forms of art you’re drawn to, what you enjoy

6th House

  • House of service and health
  • Organization, routines, fitness, diet, service to others
  • The choices you make in daily life to take care of yourself
  • How you approach day-to-day affairs and work you don't get paid for (ex. chores)

7th House

  • Rules partnerships of any kind: romantic, business, etc.
  • The type of company you seek out in others
  • Qualities you don’t embrace in yourself
  • How you cooperate with others

8th House

  • Rules intimacy, secrets, transformation, depths
  • Where you need to learn to surrender control
  • How you share resources and power
  • How you transform yourself and grow

9th House

  • Rules expansive thinking, higher-learning, morals, and beliefs
  • The experiences we need to find wisdom
  • How you relate to society and structures
  • What you seek to understand about the world

10th House

  • How we take a role in our community
  • Our relationship with authority
  • What we want to achieve, the legacy we want
  • The reputation and status you have at work

11th House

  • The type of friendships and community you want
  • The goals you want to achieve with others
  • How you feel a sense of belonging
  • Your personal aspirations, hopes, and wishes

12th House

  • Rules over the subconscious; what you hide from yourself
  • The self-destructive tendencies you have, methods of escapism
  • The parts of yourself you don’t think are safe to show others
  • How you can free yourself from false self beliefs

Let’s look at some examples:

Sun in 4th house: Your pride and energy may mostly go into your personal life, home, and family. You can be a naturally private person, feeling most yourself when at home or around people you consider family.

Mars in 9th house: You may especially enjoy the challenge of learning new things or debating. You may put a lot of effort into your learning and be highly motivated to do so. You may be angered by people who aren’t able to pick things up as quickly as you or aren’t interested in philosophical or deeper conversations.
